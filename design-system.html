Infinite Battery

Repairable · Fire-resistant · Connected

A complete visual identity for Infinite Battery, derived from the world it operates in — measurement, materials science, and electrochemistry. The system pairs the precision of a physics journal with the warmth of long-form reading: a near-black/off-white ground, a single violet accent borrowed from the high-energy end of the spectrum, and a typographic hierarchy built for clarity over decoration. Violet replaces the danger-coded red of a typical battery brand — it signals energy and precision without alarm. Every asset built on this system should read like a well-reasoned scientific article.

Foundation

Design principles

Five rules that keep every asset coherent. When a decision is ambiguous, defer to these.

P1

Evidence over assertion

Lead with the measured fact, the figure, the spec. The brand earns trust by showing data, not by adjectives. A chart outranks a slogan.

P2

One accent, used as a signal

Violet is reserved for the single most important thing on a surface — the call to action, the key data point, the result. If everything is the accent, nothing is.

P3

Read like a paper, not a pitch

Serif body text, generous measure, real hierarchy. Captions and labels speak in the monospace "lab notebook" voice. The reader should feel informed, not sold to.

P4

Structure encodes meaning

Numbering, rules, and labels exist only where order or grouping is real — a process, a dataset, a sequence. Never as ornament.

P5

Precision in the quiet parts

This is a minimal system, so spacing, alignment, and hairline weights carry the quality. Get the millimetres right and the whole thing feels engineered.

Ratio guideline — roughly 90% paper/ink, 8% grey, 2% violet. The accent appears at most once or twice per viewport. Ink on Paper measures ~19:1 contrast; Violet on White ~7.6:1 — comfortably AA for body text and white-on-violet buttons alike, so the accent is safe to use as small text where red would have failed.

Typography

Three voices

A display sans for headers and interface, a serif for everything you actually read, and a mono for data and labels. All three are open-source (Google Fonts) — zero licensing friction across web, print, and product.

Every Infinite cell pack is built to be opened, diagnosed, and repaired — not discarded. The connected BMS reports per-cell state of health in real time, so a single weak cell can be identified and swapped rather than condemning the entire pack. This is the argument for longevity, made in plain, readable prose the way a journal makes a claim: stated once, clearly, then supported by evidence.

Mark

Logo & lockups

The mark reads as an infinity loop resolved into two orbital nodes above a charged base — continuity, connection, energy. It is a solid-fill mark: it works only in Ink, reversed White, or knocked-out of Royal Violet. Never recolor the gradient or add effects.

Don't: stretch, rotate, add shadows/gradients, place on busy photos, recolor outside the three approved treatments, or set the wordmark in any face other than Space Grotesk. Minimum size: 20px / 6mm mark height.

Signature element

The figure

This is what makes the brand unmistakable: real data, drawn as a journal figure. Hairline axes, monospace tick labels, a numbered caption below, and violet reserved for the result — sitting open on the page, never in a card. Reuse this exact style everywhere — web, decks, datasheets, spec PDFs.
