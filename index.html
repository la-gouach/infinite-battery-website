Method

What we build, why, and how

What

We build battery packs as serviceable systems, not sealed consumables. Cell-level electronics, a connected BMS, and a mechanical design meant to be opened — every pack traceable from assembly to end of life.

Why

A battery is the most expensive and shortest-lived part of a light EV. Sealing it shut throws away the pack when only one part has failed — while most cells are still healthy. Repairability turns a disposable cost into a recoverable asset: lower TCO for fleets, far less waste.

How

Each cell is monitored individually and balanced by an embedded BMS running our own firmware. A fire-protection architecture — aluminum casing, intumescent coating, pressure valve — contains thermal events. Each pack reports over the air: pack and cell-level voltage, temperatures, state of charge, humidity, firmware version, maintenance history, and geographic position.

Operations

A repair network, not a repair service

Repairability only matters if anyone can act on it. We don't run repairs as a centralized service — we provide the hardware, the software, and the knowledge so that others can.

Every pack is designed to be opened and serviced with standard tools. A failed cell group is replaced, the pack is re-tested to specification, and its full history is logged against its individual telemetry record. Fleet operators can do this in-house through our short online training, or rely on a growing network of independent technicians.

The result is an open layer rather than a closed service: brands, fleets, and independents can all plug in. We build the backbone for a world where batteries are repaired and recovered instead of replaced — and let an ecosystem grow on top of it.
